Teaching Philosophy

Teaching is not just a professional responsibility for me; it is a deeply personal and fulfilling part of my academic journey. I strive to foster an inclusive and engaging classroom where every student feels both supported and challenged.

My teaching emphasizes adaptability: I tailor my approach to student needs, whether through technology, diverse assessment methods, or scaffolding complex material. I encourage collaborative learning and open dialogue, where mistakes are valued as opportunities for growth.

Reflection is central to my practice. I actively seek feedback from students and peers, and I adapt my methods based on what resonates most. By creating a space where curiosity thrives, I hope to empower students not only to master material, but also to grow as independent thinkers and problem-solvers.

Ultimately, teaching is about connection. I aim to make mathematics accessible, relevant, and inspiring, while supporting diverse learners on their academic journeys.
